# Your First Track

This tutorial walks you through building a simple four-bar techno loop in modecat_,
then saving it to your account so you can pick it up later on any device.

**Time:** about 10 minutes  
**Requires:** a modecat account ([sign up free](https://app.modecat.net/auth/signup))

---

## 1. Open the tracker

Go to [modecat.net](https://modecat.net). You'll see the main grid — rows are instruments
(kick, snare, hi-hat, bass…), columns are 16th-note steps.

## 2. Set the BPM

Click the **BPM** field at the top of the screen and type `140`. This is a solid tempo for
techno. Press Enter to confirm.

## 3. Program the kick

Find the **Kick** row. Click every **4th** step to place a kick drum on each beat:

```
Steps:  1  .  .  .  5  .  .  .  9  .  .  .  13  .  .  .
Kick:   ●  ○  ○  ○  ●  ○  ○  ○  ●  ○  ○  ○   ●  ○  ○  ○
```

Hit **Play** (spacebar) to hear it.

## 4. Add a snare

In the **Snare** row, place hits on steps **5** and **13** (beats 2 and 4):

```
Steps:  1  .  .  .  5  .  .  .  9  .  .  .  13  .  .  .
Snare:  ○  ○  ○  ○  ●  ○  ○  ○  ○  ○  ○  ○   ●  ○  ○  ○
```

## 5. Layer a hi-hat

In the **Hi-hat** row, place hits on every **even** step for a driving 8th-note pattern:

```
Steps:  1  .  3  .  5  .  7  .  9  .  11  .  13  .  15  .
HH:     ●  ○  ●  ○  ●  ○  ●  ○  ●  ○   ●  ○   ●  ○   ●  ○
```

## 6. Extend to four bars

Click the **Length** selector and choose **4 bars**. The grid expands to 64 steps. Copy your
existing 16-step pattern across all four bars using the **duplicate** button (the double-page
icon next to the bar selector).

## 7. Add some variation

Pick the last bar (steps 49–64) and remove a few hi-hat hits, or add an extra snare on step
61. Small variations keep a loop from feeling too mechanical.

## 8. Save to the cloud

When you're happy with the loop:

1. Make sure you're connected to your account (see [Getting Started](/docs/getting-started)
   if you haven't done this yet).
2. Click **Save to cloud** in the toolbar.
3. Enter a name like `techno loop 140` and press **Save**.

The project appears instantly in your [Projects dashboard](https://app.modecat.net/dashboard/projects).

## 9. Add tags and BPM from the dashboard

Head to the dashboard, find your new project, and click its card to open the detail view.
Add the tags `techno` and `loop` and confirm the BPM is set to `140`. These fields make
it easier to search and filter your projects later.

---

## What next?

- Experiment with the **velocity** layer — right-click any step to set how hard it hits.
- Try the **probability** knob to add randomness to individual steps.
- Read about [Cloud Save](/docs/cloud-save) to understand how your files are stored.
